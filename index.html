San Francisco Crime Data

A data-driven exploration of crime patterns (2003–2025)

This project explores patterns in San Francisco crime data over the past 20 years. Through interactive visualizations, we uncover trends across time, location, and crime type.

We use crime data from the years 2003 to 2025, obtained from two datasets provided by the San Francisco Police Department through DataSF. These datasets were merged into one, requiring several modifications.

Some crime categories were originally classified differently, and variables such as date formats needed to be standardized. After cleaning and preparing the data, we selected nine crime categories that were both relevant and consistent across datasets.

Through our analysis, we developed a strong understanding of the dataset and identified several interesting patterns. In particular, drug-related crimes stood out due to notable trends (xxx).

This report explores these findings in more depth, with a focus on uncovering meaningful insights from the data.
